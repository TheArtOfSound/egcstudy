Expression-Gated Consciousness: An Empirical Study

Expressive suppression and psychological transparency — Qira LLC, Phoenix, Arizona

Principal Investigators

Bryan Leonard and Brandyn Leonard · Qira LLC, Phoenix, Arizona

Purpose of the Study

This study examines how the act of expressing thoughts and feelings affects psychological clarity and self-awareness. You will be asked to write briefly about a personally meaningful topic and answer questions about how expression affects your inner experience. The study takes approximately five to ten minutes.

What Participation Involves

  • Reading a brief prompt and writing a short response (two to five sentences)
  • Answering questions about your experience of expression and suppression
  • All responses are anonymous — no name, email, or identifying information is collected
  • Your anonymous response data will be used in academic research on consciousness and expression

Voluntary Participation

Participation is completely voluntary. You may stop at any time without consequence. There is no penalty for not participating or for withdrawing.

Risks

This study involves reflection on personal emotional experiences. Some participants may find this mildly uncomfortable. If you feel distressed at any point, you may stop immediately. This study is not therapy and the researchers are not clinicians.

Benefits

There is no direct benefit to you for participating. The research contributes to scientific understanding of how expression shapes conscious experience.

Confidentiality

Your responses are completely anonymous. No identifying information is collected. Data is stored securely and will only be reported in aggregate or as anonymized case examples in academic publications.

Data Use

Anonymous response data may be published in peer-reviewed academic journals, presented at conferences, and used to inform further research on consciousness and expression.

Contact

Write freely

Task 2 of 2 · Evaluated Expression

Write for an audience

Same topic — different context.

Your writing will be scored and compared to other participants.

Describe the same place you wrote about before. This time, your writing will be analyzed and scored for expressive richness, clarity of thought, and authenticity of perspective. Your score will be compared to other participants, and you will receive your percentile ranking when the study is complete. Write your best, most considered response.
